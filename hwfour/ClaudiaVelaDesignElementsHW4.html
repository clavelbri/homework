Design Elements Homework 4

1. The Keith Haring Foundation website - Contrast

The Keith Haring Foundation website is a good example of the use of contrast:

  • The colors on the main page create effective contrast that is representative of
    Keith Haring's artwork



  • The colors red, black and white pop against one another and capture the viewer's attention



  • The use of text boxes with sharp corners create visual tension against the smooth edges of
    Haring's characters in his paintings



  • The combination of large bold red text and smaller dark gray text also add good contrast





2. Easy Plant website - Repetition

The Easy Plant website uses repetition to maintain consistency in their design style:

  1. The muted color pallete and filter is repeated throughout the website



  2. The repetition of the same font makes the website simple and easy to navigate


  3. The images of the plants all stick to the same size, pallete, filter and similar background style


  4. The repetition of all these elements help to create identity and unique style



3. Good Books website - Alignment

Goodbooks.io uses alignment to give the website a sense of organization:
-Left-alignment of text elements can be seen all throught the website,
including the individual book description pages.


Edge alignment is also used:
-Images of books and people featured on the website are positioned
against a margin that matches up with their outer edges



The Goodbooks logo uses left-side alignment as well as repetition:
- Each letter on the two lines of the logo is aligned, the "s" on the word
"books" hangs off to the right but is balanced by the empty space above
the block design



4. Host Defense website - Proximity

The Host Defense website makes good use of proximity in its design,
making it easy for users to navigate through and find the information they need:

  1. The white space between Host Defense's logo and the menu options on the right
    effectively separate the logo, giving it the presence it needs and also grouping the hyperlinks


  2. The website effectively groups like topics together and has plenty of white space
    between the groups. This white space is very important to give the site a clean and
    organized look for a better user experience.


  3. Different product types are grouped with the description below their image and a "shop" button,
    this gives the customer confidence that they are purchasing the right product
